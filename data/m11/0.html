# MATH 1120 - Midterm II Produc rule > $$ (fg)' = f'g + fg' $$ Quotient rule > $$ \left( \frac{f}{g} \right)' = \frac{f'g - fg'}{g^2} $$
The first term has the derivative of the top, the denominator is the square of the original denominator.
Chain rule > $$ (f(g(x)))' = f'(g(x))g'(x) $$
Don't forget the final g'(x)
Trig identities > $$ \sin^2 x + \cos^2 x = 1 $$ $$ \sin 2\theta = 2\sin x \cos x $$ $$ \cos 2\theta = \cos^2 x - \sin^2 x $$ Limit of sine > $$ \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin x}{x} = 1 $$

The reciprocal also equals 1

Derivative of trig functions > $$\begin{aligned} \sin x &\rightarrow \cos x & \cos x &\rightarrow -\sin x \\ \tan x &\rightarrow \sec^2 x & \cot x &\rightarrow -\csc^2 x \\ \sec x &\rightarrow \sec x \tan x & \csc x &\rightarrow -\csc x \cot x \end{aligned}$$
  • Despite sin has the reciprocal csc, here it is on the same colume as sec
  • The derivative of the "opposite" functions are simply just a negative.
Derivative of inverse trig functions > $$\begin{aligned} \sin^{-1} x &\rightarrow \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^2}} & \cos^{-1} x &\rightarrow -(\sin^{-1})' \\ \tan^{-1} x &\rightarrow \frac{1}{1+x^2} & \cot^{-1} x &\rightarrow -(\tan^{-1})' \\ \sec^{-1} x &\rightarrow \frac{1}{x\sqrt{x^2-1}} & \csc^{-1} x &\rightarrow -(\csc^{-1})' \end{aligned}$$

The derivative of the "opposite" function is just the negative of the opposite function.

It is good idea to break down functions into sine and cosine, but answer in a combined function (for solving)
Exponential function > $$ (a^x)' = a^x \times \ln x $$ $$ (\log_b x)' = \frac{1}{x}\frac{1}{\ln b} $$
It is generally helpful to breakdown complex equation into many terms, as long as they don't contain product, quotient, or power (can be removed using log rules).
